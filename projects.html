My Projects

1. Digital Tachometer

The Digital Tachometer project was developed and presented in my department function as a practical innovation to measure rotational speed with improved accuracy. This tachometer uses optical sensing to detect revolutions per minute (RPM) of a rotating object, converting them into digital readings displayed on an LCD. I designed this with cost-effectiveness, accuracy, and simplicity in mind. It reflects my strong interest in sensor interfacing, signal processing, and real-time embedded systems.

2. Ultimate Line Tracer

This project was showcased at the prestigious Kurukshetra 2025 event hosted by the College of Engineering, Guindy. It involved building an autonomous robot capable of following a designated path using line-detection sensors. The robot was designed for optimal speed and stability, using precise control systems. This project challenged my embedded programming skills and gave me deep insight into motor drivers, feedback systems, and microcontroller integration. It strengthened my problem-solving and innovation abilities under competitive conditions.
